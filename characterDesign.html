CHARACTER DESIGN

For our character design task, we had the complete freedom of inventing a character from scratch (preferrably a character for a game). After creating a mindmap and brainstorming ideas that I liked, I chose to create a nonbinary hunter/assassin. Here you'll find a quick overview of my process, as well as screenshots and a PDF folio of my work!


CHARACTER DESIGN FOLIO


Silhouette Sketches: To help me get a feel of the character, as well as explore different possible poses I could draw my character in, I experimented by drawing 20 different silhouette poses. I then chose 5 of my favourites and refined them.

Detailed Pose: Choosing one of my 5 final silhouette drawings, I drew Artemis for the first time! This was just a sketch to get the pose downpat, but it also helped to add detail and get a clear idea of what I wanted Artemis to look like.


Orthographic: An orthographic drawing is when you draw a character from the front view and side view/back view (some people draw front and side, others draw front and back). This helpes designers to understand the exact details of the clothing and appearance of the character.


Colour Pallettes: I was required to show a demonstration of different colour pallettes for Artemis, consisting of split complimentary, complimentary, and triad colours.


Final Colours: After experimenting with different colours, I decided on this as the final colours for Artemis. The colours represented their adventures in the game, and how they grew up in the jungle/forest.


Final Poster: This is my final character design poster! I took the front final coloured orthographic and put it in front of a lightened grey background with the characters name at the top.
