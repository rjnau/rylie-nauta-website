Artemis Synposis

Set in an alternate universe filled with ancient creatures of the Triassic, Jurassic and Cretaceous periods of the Mesozoic era, you play as a huntress named Artemis. The Earth is split into 3 large continents; Triassica, Jurassica and Cretaceia, each filled with the animals from each Mesozoic period. The player, or Artemis, begins their journey in the Triassic continent, Triassica, where their tribe has been wiped out by a strange and dangerous creature which had disappeared just as mysteriously as it had appeared. Artemis must complete quests and rebuild civilisation with an ambitious amount of side quests and main quests. Collect resources, craft tools, befriend tribes, tame creatures, build villages, discover landmarks, gather resources, escape angry tribes; Artemis’ adventures are endless.


Behind The Scenes

Artemis began with the design of the protagonist, Artemis. From there, a world and story behind Artemis just came to life; I thought, ‘How cool would it be if people got to experience Artemis’ adventure themselves?’.
After working on the design of the character, the world, the enemies, and all the inner workings of the story and narrative, my team and I began working on the practical of the game. Where would the player begin? Would there be cut-scenes? What would the first quests be? What assets do we need to start work on?
Beginning the asset design and models were quite easy, it was putting them all together in a game format that was actually playable that was hard. We used Maya to model, and Unreal Engine 4 to build the environment of the game itself.


Art Style

The style behind Artemis took great inspiration from the Legend of Zelda series games (more specifically, Breath of the Wild and Skyward Sword). The designs and models are realistic yet simplistic, a mixture of cartoonistic and lifelike appearances. This not only provided the game with a comfortable look and feel, it made the modelling and texturing process much easier.


Time and Budget

The ideas and planning process lasted just under two months. It seems like a while, but I had the job of designing and planning the characters, environment, story and plot, style of the game, enemies the player would face, working out finances and timelines of work.
After this process, the REAL work for everything practical with the game took my team and I around a year and a half (this was for the planning and creation of assets and extra 3D models). The following year and a half after was spent putting our game world together and working on programming, with another few months after spent working on gathering music, sound effects, voice acting --pretty much all of the audio-- and putting that into the game. The games overall budget was $500,000.
Finally, after a long lasting amount of work and effort, Artemis is set to available for purchase on October 19, 2021 on Steam, Xbox and PlayStation.
